SMS Text Messaging — Opt-In & Consent

Sunday Care Schedule · operated by xlSigma, LLC (program: "Papi Jim Caretakers") · Last updated: June 16, 2026

Program name: Sunday Care Schedule.
What messages we send: SMS reminders to family caregivers about their assigned Sunday elder-care shift (6:00-8:00 PM) for our family member, plus the related opt-in confirmation, help, and opt-out replies. This is a private tool used by one family to coordinate eldercare. We do not send marketing or promotional messages.

How caregivers opt in (Call to Action)

Consent is collected directly from each caregiver through a two-step (double opt-in) process:

  1. Permission & enrollment. With the caregiver's permission, the family organizer enters the caregiver's first name and mobile number in the caregiver management screen of the app and confirms the caregiver agreed to be contacted. (The app is a private, gated family tool; a screenshot of this consent step is shown below and also published in our Privacy Policy.)
  2. Caregiver confirmation (the binding opt-in). The app then automatically sends the caregiver a one-time confirmation text. The caregiver must reply YES from their own phone before any reminders are sent:
    "Sunday Care Schedule: you've been added to receive reminders for Papi's Sunday care shift (6-8 PM). Reply YES to confirm, STOP to opt out. Msg & data rates may apply. 1 msg/week. Privacy: https://papi-sundaygap.netlify.app/privacy-policy.html"
    A caregiver who does not reply YES, or who replies STOP, receives no further messages.

Privacy

Phone numbers are used only to send these reminders and are transmitted only to our SMS provider (Twilio) to deliver them. No mobile information is shared with third parties or affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes, and text-messaging opt-in data and consent are not shared with any third parties.
